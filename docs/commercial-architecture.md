# Commercial Architecture

## Four-Tier SKU Structure

| Tier | SKU Name | Scope | Deliverable | Typical Engagement |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — Diagnostic | **PRIMER Process Readiness Snapshot** (SP-9) | 3–5 nominated processes scored against the readiness standard; one claimed-vs-performed spot check | 10–15 page scored snapshot + gap register + bridge memo | 1–2 weeks |
| 2 — Core | **PRIMER Readiness Assessment** | Department-scope census (SP-1) + SOP records for top 8–12 processes (SP-2) + decision-logic and data audit (SP-3/SP-4) + matrix (SP-7) | 30–50 page assessment + SOP register | 4–6 weeks |
| 3 — Full Build | **PRIMER Enterprise Process Census** | Full-scope SP-1–SP-8: whole-of-business census, complete SOP register, metrics baselines, value-stream map, gated automation sequence | 60–100 page census + living SOP register + roadmap | 8–12 weeks |
| 4 — Retainer | **PRIMER Process Governance Retainer** | SP-10 operated continuously: register maintenance, quarterly attestations, new-process onboarding, pre-deployment gate reviews | Quarterly governance report + maintained register | Ongoing |

The tier ladder is the relationship design: Tier 1 opens the door, Tiers 2–3
build the asset, Tier 4 keeps it alive — and an SOP register decays back into
tribal knowledge within two quarters without it, which is why Tier 4 exists.

## Margin Protection

Tier 1's scope lock is the margin: 3–5 processes, no census, no value-stream
map, one spot check. Scope creep in a fixed-price diagnostic is priced at
zero and delivered at cost. The bridge memo is where expansion belongs.

## Deployment Modes

- **Internal Lab** — run inside a BHIL Claude Project for advisory clients;
  outputs to branded `.docx` (via `bhil-docx`) and `.md`.
- **Commercial SKU** — sold standalone as a fixed-scope engagement (Tier 1–4).
- **Retainer Anchor** — Tier 1 entry → Tier 2–3 build-up → Tier 4 recurring.
- **Partnership** — white-labelled to fractional-COO/CDO practices, MSPs, and
  automation integrators needing a defensible pre-sales readiness instrument.

## Activation Triggers

| Trigger Heard | Recommended Tier | Companion Framework |
|---|---|---|
| "We want to deploy AI agents — where do we start?" | 1 → 2 | AI Enablement & Team Intelligence |
| "We tried automating X and it made things worse" | 2 (SP-2/SP-3 heavy) | COUNSEL |
| "Document our SOPs before we scale / sell / hire" | 3 | CODEX |
| "Which of our processes should we automate first?" | 2 | Custom GPT & AI Agent Builder |
| "Board wants an AI strategy with substance" | 2 or 3 | COUNSEL · PATHFINDER |
| "Our data is a mess and we know it" | 2 (SP-4 heavy) | NEXUS |
| "Prove the automation actually worked" | 1 + SP-5 re-baseline | VERDICT |

## Companion Pairings

- **PRIMER → AI Enablement & Team Intelligence** — process readiness feeds
  team-capability planning; together they are the complete pre-deployment
  picture.
- **PRIMER → Custom GPT & AI Agent Builder** — the SP-2 SOP record and SP-3
  decision-logic register are the direct build inputs for agent construction.
- **PRIMER → COUNSEL** — the assessment slots into COUNSEL's Diagnose and
  Map phases.
- **NEXUS ↔ PRIMER** — SP-4 findings escalate to NEXUS when the data
  architecture is the engagement.
- **VERDICT ↔ PRIMER** — post-deployment claims audited against the SP-5
  baseline register.
